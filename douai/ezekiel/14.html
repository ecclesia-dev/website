Ezekiel — Chapter 14

Douai-Rheims 1609 · Annotations

These annotations are from the original 1609 Douay Old Testament, the first complete English Catholic Bible translation, produced by English scholars in exile at the English College of Douai. The archaic spelling is preserved.

Verses annotated
  1. 1
  2. 3
  3. 9
  4. 14
  5. 20
  6. 22

Verse 1

God reuealed to the Prophet that these men came not sincerely to learne, but were setled in their harts to serue the idols. As worldly men in heretical countries doe inquire of Catholike Priests what they should doe, but remaine resolued to participate with heretikes.

Verse 3

Such men are first of al to be admonished to depart from idolatrie, heresie, schisme, & from al practice thereof: which is the first step of true conuersion to God.

Verse 9

God permitteth false Prophets to be deceiued, & to deceiue, in punishment of their great sinnes, and of the people that heare them. 3. Reg. 22. v 19. 2. Par. 18. v. 18.

Verse 14

Daniel then liuing, Noe & Iob departed from this life did sometime pray for the people.

Verse 20

Noe is named for example of spiritual Pastours of the Church, Daniel of al religious orders, & Iob of holy laie people. S. Greg. l.1. c.1.

Verse 22

There shal alwayes be Pastours to bring forth & feede spiritual children of God.
