Ezechiel — Chapter 16

Douai-Rheims 1609 · Annotations

These annotations are from the original 1609 Douay Old Testament, the first complete English Catholic Bible translation, produced by English scholars in exile at the English College of Douai. The archaic spelling is preserved.

⚠ Note on Chapter & Verse Numbers

This content was digitized from the original 1609 Douay (Old Testament) and 1582 Rheims (New Testament) print editions by OCR. The OCR process sometimes confused print page numbers with verse numbers, and may have assigned annotations to the wrong chapter. Chapter and verse labels on this page reflect the OCR output from the original print pagination and may not correspond to canonical Scripture chapter/verse numbers. For canonical reference, consult a standard Douay-Rheims edition. The annotation texts themselves are authentic 1609/1582 Douay-Rheims content.

Verse 2

Ezechiel was now in Babylon, & therfore this admonition which he should giue to Ierusalem was to be notified there by letters, & messengers sent thither for this purpose.

Verse 3

By al this is signified that God made the Israelites of a barbarous nation to be ciuil, and gaue them not only things necessarie, but also ornaments, aboue the state of other nations, especially in spiritual benefites, giuing a Law, with Sacrifices, Sacraments, and other holy rites.

Verse 20

Adulterous women doe deceiue their husbands, bringing them other mens children: but the Iewes gaue their lawful children to the worst adulterers, sacrificing them to idols. 4. Reg. 6.17.21.23.

Verse 34

Al fornication is abominable, but that is most detestable when women giue rewards to men for fornication or adultrie.

Verse 45

As Ierusalem was wont to be wicked in former times, so it is now.

Verse 49

Ambition, gluttonie, & idlenes are cause of much more sinnes; temperance & labour bring forth much good fruit.

Verse 55

By Sodom & other cities are vnderstood al nations, which shal come to Christ.

Verse 57

After that al other nations are conuerted the Iewes shal also returne to Christ. Ro. 11.

Verse 60

Al gentils elder & yonger shal be conuerted to Christ.

Verse 62

Not by the couenant made with the Iewes in the Law of Moyses, but by the Euangelical couenant.
