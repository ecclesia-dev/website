Proverbs — Chapter 1

Douai-Rheims 1609 · Annotations

These annotations are from the original 1609 Douay Old Testament, the first complete English Catholic Bible translation, produced by English scholars in exile at the English College of Douai. The archaic spelling is preserved.

Verse 2

VVifdom.) As wel in thefe Sapiential books,as in other holie Scriptures, and sacred writers, the word wifdom hath three fignifications. Sometimes it importeth the Diuine Attribute called Gods wifdom; fometimes fupernatural wifdom giuen to men by the Holie Ghoft; and fometimes it fignifieth mere humane wifdom, gotten by the natural light of reafon and mans induftrie. The firft, as likewife other Diuine Attributes, Gods Power, Goodnes, Iuftic:, Truth, Mercie, and the like, are not qualities, or other accidents in God, as the fame termes fignifie in creatures, but his Diuine Subftance and Effence, whofe diuers Excellencies are called by fuch names as mans capacitie can better conceiue: and fo Gods wifdom is God himfelfe: and is appropriated to the fecond Perfon of the bleffed Trinitie, as Power is approprieted to God the Father, and Goodnes ro the Holie Ghoft.

Verse 24

Because I called and you refused.) God voutfaffeth foure benefits of grace to euerie man, al neceffarie and fufficient for his faluation: 1. He calleth al by preaching, or good infpiration. 2. He offereth help. 3. He inftructeth the ignorant what is good, that they may choofe it if they wil. 4. And reprehendeth euil, that they may fhun it. They therfore that neglect this manifold grace in this life, fhal without al remedie be damned, being too late to repent in the other world. For then they fhal crie and not be heard.

Verse 33

But he that heareth me.) Contrariwise thofe that accept Gods grace, and cooperate therewith, fhal haue eternal reft and ioy.
