2 Machabees — Chapter 15

Douai-Rheims 1609 · Annotations

These annotations are from the original 1609 Douay Old Testament, the first complete English Catholic Bible translation, produced by English scholars in exile at the English College of Douai. The archaic spelling is preserved.

Verse 1

Prophane men make their advantage of religious mens good conscience, but Iudas rightly instructed defended his just cause also in the Sabbath.

Verse 12

Onias prayed for al the people. And Ieremie prayeth for the people. Seeing the Prophet Ieremie, and the High-Priest Onias, being in Limbo patrum, did pray for the whole people of the Iewes, it is also certaine that they, and other Saints in glorie, doe of their excellent charitie pray for those that are in this mortal life. S. Bernard admonisheth that a good religious man is like to this Onias who prayeth to God for al the people.

Verse 14

Ieremie persuaded the King to yeald himself to the Chaldees, and not to resist. But now he delivereth a sword to Iudas, exhorting him to fight: according to Gods wil in divers cases, for there is a time of warre, and a time of peace. Eccle. 3.

Verse 18

Gods honour and holie things are first and principally to be respected, before worldlie freinds, though they also must be regarded in due order and place.

Verse 39

If not so worthily.] He demandeth not pardon, as though he suspected any errour in his doctrine or in the history, but of his vnpolished stile in writing. As S. Paul sayth that himself was rude in speach, yet not in knowledge, 1. Cor. 11. v. 6. But we, who by Gods great goodnes haue passed now to the end of this English old Testament; iustly fearing that we haue not worthily discharged so great a work: & in no wise presuming that we haue auoided al errours, as wel of doctrine as historie: much more we acknowledge that our title is rude & vnpolished. And therefore we necessarily, and with al humilitie craue pardon of God & of his glorious Saints, Likewise of the Church militant, & particularly of you, right welbeloued English readers, to whom as at the beginning we directed & dedicated these our endeuours: so to you we offer the rest of our labours, euen to the end of our liues: in our B. Sauiour IESVS Christ, to whom be al prayse and glorie. Amen.
