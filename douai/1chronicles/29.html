1 Chronicles — Chapter 29

Douai-Rheims 1609 · Annotations

These annotations are from the original 1609 Douay Old Testament, the first complete English Catholic Bible translation, produced by English scholars in exile at the English College of Douai. The archaic spelling is preserved.

Verses annotated
  1. 2
  2. 15
  3. 20
  4. 29

Verse 2

That which he had already vowed & prepared he counted not his owne, and now giueth more to the building & furnishing of the Temple.

Verse 15

As Abraham, Isaac, Iacob & others were strangers in respect of Chanaan: so both they, and also Dauid, Salomon, and al men in this world, are pilgrimes in respect of heauen. Heb. 13.

Verse 20

Albeit they did the same exteriour act of honour to God and the King: yet in their mind and intention they gaue diuine honour to God, and ciuil to the King.

Verse 29

Either Nathan and Gad write the later part of the first book of Kings, or els their books are not now extant.
