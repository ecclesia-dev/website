Wisdom — Chapter 4

Douai-Rheims 1609 · Annotations

These annotations are from the original 1609 Douay Old Testament, the first complete English Catholic Bible translation, produced by English scholars in exile at the English College of Douai. The archaic spelling is preserved.

Verses annotated
  1. 1
  2. 7
  3. 11

Verse 1

Chastitie of the bodie is a singular great vertue, & spirituall chastitie of true faith and religion is greater, and more generally commended, as the roote and foundation of al vertues. For without faith it is vnpossible to please God.

Verse 7

When fo euer the iust dieth it is profitable for him: & dying yong his immaculate life is more commendable then old age in the wicked.

Verse 11

He vvas taken avvay.) By this place S. Augustin proueth that such as died in good state, might haue fallen into wickednes, if they had liued longer: and therefore it was a benefit to them to dye fooner. Which allured foreknowledge of al things that shal be, or may be, standeth wel with mans free-wil.
