Numbers — Chapter 36

Douai-Rheims 1609 · Annotations

These annotations are from the original 1609 Douay Old Testament, the first complete English Catholic Bible translation, produced by English scholars in exile at the English College of Douai. The archaic spelling is preserved.

Verses annotated
  1. 4

Verse 4

Distribution of lots.] By reason of two former lawes, the one (Levit. 25.) prouiding that inheritance of lande, should not be fold, nor otherwise alienated, but vntil the Iubilee yeare, and then returne to him, or his heyres, to whom it pertained before; the other (Num. 27.) ordaining that for lack of a sonne, daughters should enherite; this difficultie did rise, in case an enheretrixe did marrie a man of an other tribe, her lands by that meanes should passe from tribe to tribe, and not be restored in the Iubilee yeare. For auoiding of which inconuenience a further law is made, that none shal marrie out of their owne tribe. Neuerthlesse the tribe of Leui made mariage with the tribe of Iuda: as appeareth by that Zacharie the Priest married Elizabeth cosin to our B. Ladie of the tribe of Iuda; though in the old Testament there is no such expresse dispensation, nor explication of the law, but by tradition was holden for lawful and practised by so holie a man as Zacharie. And not without mysterie (as S. Augustin noteth li. 2. c. 2. de consen. Euang.) for that Christ the Annointed of God, was prefigured by the annointing of Kings and Priests, and borne of the royal and priestlie tribes, being both a King and a Priest.
