Jeremiah — Chapter 26

Cornelius à Lapide SJ · Biblical Commentary

Synopsis: Jeremiah's Temple Speech (parallel to ch.7) lands him before the princes for trial on a charge of death. He is defended by Ahikam and the memory of Micah's precedent. A contrast with the prophet Uriah who was killed. Lapide: the chapter illustrates the prophetic courage that prefers death to false prophecy.
