Genesis — Chapter 25

Cornelius à Lapide SJ · Biblical Commentary

Verse 8

AND BEING OLD AND FULL OF DAYS, HE DIED IN A GOOD OLD AGE, AND WAS GATHERED TO HIS PEOPLE. — Abraham died at 175 years. \"Full of days\" = satisfied with life, longing for eternal life, fully completed in his course. \"Gathered to his people\" = reunited with the souls of the just in the realm of the departed. Isaac and Ishmael, reconciled, buried their father together — a touching sign of filial piety and of Ishmael's conversion to the worship of the true God in his later years (Lapide, citing Josephus).

Verse 23

AND THE LORD SAID TO HER: TWO NATIONS ARE IN THY WOMB, AND TWO PEOPLES SHALL BE DIVIDED OUT OF THY WOMB, AND ONE PEOPLE SHALL OVERCOME THE OTHER, AND THE ELDER SHALL SERVE THE YOUNGER. — The oracle at the twins' birth: one of the clearest pre-birth prophecies in Scripture. Paul cites this (Rom. 9:12) as the foundational text of the gratuity of divine election: \"not of works but of Him who calleth.\" Jacob was chosen not because of foreknown merits but by God's free election. Lapide refutes Calvin: God's election is not arbitrary but based on His foreknowledge of what each would freely choose; yet the election itself was prior to any works. The fulfillment: Edomites (Esau's descendants) were long dominant; but under David and the Israelites (Jacob's descendants) they became subjects — literally fulfilled (2 Kings 8:14; 1 Mach. 5:65).

Verse 29

AND JACOB BOILED POTTAGE: AND ESAU CAME FROM THE FIELD FAINT. — Esau sells his birthright for a mess of red lentil pottage. Lapide: \"Of all the sins and follies of men, this may be the greatest type: to exchange eternal goods for momentary pleasures.\" Hebrews call him \"Edom\" (red) from this moment — the red pottage being the symbol of earthly appetite triumphing over heavenly dignity. Chrysostom: \"Thus still do men who sell Christ for momentary pleasure, for money, for reputation, for carnal comfort.\"
