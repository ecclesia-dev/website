Calvin vs. Bellarmine: Five Disputations

The Reformation divided Western Christendom on five fundamental questions: justification, authority, predestination, the Eucharist, and Purgatory. These disputations present the strongest version of each side — Calvin for the Reformed position, Bellarmine for the Catholic — so that the reader may weigh the arguments and judge where the truth lies. The format is adversarial because the truth has nothing to fear from a fair fight.

I. Sola Fide

Justification by faith alone — does God save the sinner by declaring him righteous through faith, or by making him righteous through grace that transforms and demands cooperation?

II. Sola Scriptura

Scripture alone as the rule of faith — is the Bible the only infallible authority, or does Sacred Tradition interpreted by the Magisterium hold equal weight?

III. Predestination

Election, reprobation, and the will of God — did God unconditionally decree who would be saved and who would be damned before the foundation of the world?

Real Presence and the sacrifice of the Mass — do the bread and wine become the Body and Blood of Christ, and is the Mass a true propitiatory sacrifice?
