## ⛔ HOLD — INACCURATE SAINT DESCRIPTION FLAGGED

### Flagged Claim — rome-indulgence-guide.html, section #seven-churches

**Text in article:**
> "Around 1553, Philip Neri — then not yet a priest, a layman devoting himself to works of charity
> in Rome — began organizing nocturnal pilgrimages to the seven ancient pilgrim churches."

**The problem:**
Philip Neri was ordained a priest on **May 23, 1551**. The article states he was "not yet a priest, a layman" in "Around 1553." This is factually incorrect — by 1553, Neri had been a priest for approximately two years.

**What is accurate:**
- Neri arrived in Rome in 1534.
- He spent approximately 17 years (1534–1551) as a layman devoting himself to works of charity.
- He organized early visits to the Seven Churches *during his years as a layman* — likely in the late 1540s or very early 1550s before his ordination.
- He was ordained 23 May 1551.
- The organized nocturnal pilgrimages as a structured counter-Carnivale program developed gradually; some sources date the formalized version to 1552 or 1553, by which point he was already a priest.

**The error in the source markdown (from Isidore):** The date "around 1553" and the description "not yet a priest, a layman" cannot both be true. Either the date should be earlier (late 1540s, when he was still a layman), or the description should read "then newly ordained" or simply omit the lay/priest distinction.

**Required correction (do not fix — flagging for Isidore/Tim):**
Option A: Change "Around 1553" to "In the late 1540s" (before his 1551 ordination)  
Option B: Change "then not yet a priest, a layman" to "devoting himself to works of charity"  
One of these corrections must be made to accurately describe St. Philip Neri.
